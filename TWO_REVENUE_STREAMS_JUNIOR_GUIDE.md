## The Big Picture First

Imagine you're really good at building LEGO cities.

You have **two ways to make money from the same skill:**

1. **Someone pays you $15,000 to come to their house and build a custom LEGO city for them.** You design it, build it, set it up, and hand them the keys. Done. They own it.

2. **That same client, once they see their city working, pays you $3,000/month to keep it running** -- adding new sections, maintaining it, making it better every month. They keep paying as long as the value keeps coming.

Same LEGO skill. Two revenue streams. One relationship. **Both are active at the same time from the same client.**

That's exactly what you have with PVRPOSE AI OS.

## Part 1: Your Two Revenue Streams (From the Same Product)

### Revenue Stream 1: The Build
**The one-time fee for designing and deploying the system**

This is where the relationship starts. A founder pays you to come in, understand their business, and build a custom AI operating system over 6 weeks. When you hand it off, they own it completely. It runs on their infrastructure. You have no ongoing obligation unless they choose a retainer.

**Who pays for this:**
Business owners making $100K+ per year who are losing 10+ hours/week to admin. They want it done FOR them. They don't have time to figure it out.

**What they pay:**
- $2,500 CAD -- AI OS Readiness Audit (2-week blueprint and ROI analysis)
- $15,000-$25,000 CAD -- Full custom build (6 weeks)
- $5,000-$7,500 CAD -- Standard audit rate (post-pioneer)

**The relationship:** Close collaboration for 6-8 weeks. You ask deep questions about their business, their voice, their workflows. You build it around exactly how they operate.

---

### Revenue Stream 2: The Retainer
**The recurring monthly income from clients who want the system to keep growing**

This is where the recurring revenue lives. After the build is delivered, many clients want:
- A new automation built each month
- Ongoing optimization as their business changes
- A monthly strategy call to prioritize what to build next
- Someone to call when something breaks

This is the retainer. It converts a one-time project into a long-term relationship and predictable monthly income.

**Who pays for this:**
Same clients as Stream 1 -- the ones who saw the value of the build and want to keep going.

**What they pay:**
- From $3,000 CAD/month
- Includes: 1 new automation/month, optimization, monthly strategy call, async support

**The relationship:** Ongoing monthly. 8-12 hours of work per client per month. Highly leveraged because all the setup is done -- you are just improving an existing system.

## Part 2: Why These Two Streams Work Together

### They Are Not Separate -- They Are Sequential

Every client goes through the same lifecycle:

```
AWARENESS (LinkedIn content, TNC network, referral)
    |
    v
DISCOVERY CALL (qualify + audit offer)
    |
    v
AUDIT ($2,500-$7,500 CAD)
    |
    v
FULL BUILD ($15,000-$25,000 CAD)
    |
    v
RETAINER ($3,000+/month, ongoing)
    |
    v
REFERRAL + TESTIMONIAL (feeds the next client)
```

**Stream 1 creates Stream 2:**
- You build the system. The client sees it save them 12 hours/week.
- They want to keep adding to it. They sign the retainer.
- You now have $3,000+/month from a client you already fully onboarded.

**Stream 2 funds the time to sell Stream 1:**
- With 3 retainer clients ($9,000/month), you have a revenue floor that covers base expenses.
- You can take fewer but better discovery calls. You stop needing to close every deal.
- Better pipeline = better clients = better testimonials = more referrals.

This is the flywheel. It compounds once it starts.

## Part 6: The Discovery Call (How to Run It)

When someone books a call from your landing page or through TNC, run this:

### The 30-Minute Discovery Script

**Minutes 0-5: Understand their situation**
- "Tell me about your business -- what do you do, for how long, how many clients?"
- "What does a typical Monday morning look like for you?"
- Listen for: admin friction, things they keep putting off, where they lose time

**Minutes 5-15: Find the specific pain**
- "How many hours per week go to things like proposals, follow-ups, briefings?"
- "What's the most frustrating thing you do repeatedly that someone else could handle if they knew your business well?"
- "If you got 10 hours back per week, what would you actually do with them?"
- Listen for: concrete pain (proposals, follow-ups, context loss), dollar value of the time

**Minutes 15-20: Show the specific solution**
- "What you just described -- the proposal bottleneck -- is exactly what the system handles first. A client I built this for went from 3-hour proposals to 20 minutes. From their phone."
- Show the demo if you have it. Or describe the morning digest use case in detail.
- "Your voice, your clients, your workflows. Not a template -- a system built around how you specifically operate."

**Minutes 20-25: Qualify the fit**
- "To make sure this is the right fit -- are you at $100K+ revenue annually?"
- "Are you comfortable with a 2-week audit first so we can map exactly what to build for you?"
- "The audit is $2,500 CAD, fully credited toward the build if you proceed."

**Minutes 25-30: Close or next step**
- If yes: "I can start the audit [date]. I'll send you a brief proposal this afternoon."
- If unsure: "Take the free assessment on pvrpose.ai -- gives you a score and a specific roadmap."
- If not qualified: "The timing sounds like it may not be right yet. Let's reconnect when you're past [their specific blocker]."

They don't compete. They feed each other sequentially. Every build client is a potential retainer client. Every retainer client is a source of referrals for the next build.

**To get clients now:**
1. Fill 3 pioneer spots this week (personal outreach to 20 people you know)

**The business you're building:**

- Referrals = free acquisition that scales without your time

Start with the pioneer spots. Everything else follows from excellent delivery.
